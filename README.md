## Overview

This repository constitutes a living academic and practitioner reference for the theoretical infrastructure of modern advertising and marketing. Grounded in 300 peer-reviewed and industry sources synthesized via NotebookLM, it maps classical communication and behavioral theories onto the emergent realities of **Large Language Models (LLMs)**, **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)**, **agentic AI**, and **virtual influencer ecosystems**.

The project is structured for researchers, senior strategists, and open-source contributors who require precise theoretical grounding when designing AI-informed campaigns, evaluating algorithmic persuasion systems, or navigating the compressed attention economy of 2026.

## Key Terminology Glossary

**Algorithmic Persuasion** — The deployment of machine learning systems to identify, target, and shift consumer attitudes or behaviors at scale, often operating below conscious awareness thresholds.

**CASA Paradigm (Computers Are Social Actors)** — The theoretical framework positing that humans apply social norms and expectations to computers and AI entities, enabling parasocial dynamics with virtual influencers.

**Cognitive Load Theory** — The study of mental effort required to process information, critical for optimizing AI-generated creative assets and multimodal advertising stimuli.

**Dual Process Theory** — The distinction between System 1 (fast, heuristic, emotional) and System 2 (slow, deliberative, analytical) cognition; foundational for understanding when peripheral vs. central persuasion routes dominate.

**Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** — The emerging discipline of optimizing content for inclusion in AI-generated answers from LLMs (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity), expanding beyond traditional SEO.

**Search Everywhere Optimization (SEO 2.0)** — The unified optimization strategy spanning traditional search engines, social search (TikTok, Instagram), and generative AI platforms simultaneously.

**Semantic Data Layer** — Structured, machine-interpretable data annotations that enable LLMs and search engines to accurately understand brand entity relationships, product attributes, and content intent.

**De-influencing** — A counter-cultural social media phenomenon where creators discourage consumption, complicating Attribution Theory models and forcing brand transparency recalibration.

**Schema Theory (Brand Context)** — Cognitive structures consumers use to organize brand-related knowledge; in VR/metaverse contexts, brand-schema alignment determines immersive experience coherence.

**Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB)** — Ajzen's model linking attitude, subjective norms, and perceived behavioral control to behavioral intention; now extended to AI-mediated purchase environments.
